What is the value of $$ \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-1)^{n+1}\frac{\ln(2n+1)}{2n+1}? $$

We want to find the value of $$ \beta'(1) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-1)^{n+1}\frac{\ln(2n+1)}{2n+1}. $$ where $\beta'(1)$ references the derivative of the Dirichlet beta function. We start rather distantly by first finding the Fourier series of $\ln \Gamma(x)$ over $0 < x < 1$: $$ \ln \Gamma(x) = a_{0} + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\bigg(a_{n}\cos(2\pi n x) + b_{n}\sin(2\pi nx)\bigg). $$ We compute the Fourier coefficients.

For $a_{0}$, we have $$\begin{aligned} a_{0} &= \int_{0}^{1} \ln \Gamma(x) \, \mathrm dx \\ &= \int_{0}^{1}\ln \left(\frac{\pi}{\sin(\pi x)\Gamma(1-x)}\right) \, \mathrm dx, \, \text{via gamma reflection} \\ &= \int_{0}^{1} \ln \pi \, \mathrm dx - \int_{0}^{1}\ln\sin(\pi x) \, \mathrm dx - \underbrace{\int_{0}^{1}\ln\Gamma(1-x) \, \mathrm dx}_{=a_{0}, \text{ via } t=1-x} \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\left(\ln \pi - \int_{0}^{1}\ln\sin(\pi x) \, \mathrm dx \right) \end{aligned} $$ However, $$\begin{aligned} \int_{0}^{1}\ln\sin(\pi x) \, \mathrm dx &= \frac{1}{\pi}\int_{0}^{\pi} \ln \sin t \, \mathrm dt, \, \text{via } t = \pi x \\ &= \frac{2}{\pi}\underbrace{\int_{0}^{\pi/2}\ln \sin t \, \mathrm dt}_{=-\frac{\pi}{2}\ln 2}, \, \text{via symmetry} \\ &= -\ln 2. \end{aligned} $$ where the underbraced result is notorious and has been evaluated many times, for instance here . Therefore, $$ a_{0} = \frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi). $$ For $a_{n}$, we start similarly: $$\begin{aligned} a_{n} &= 2\int_{0}^{1}\ln \Gamma(x) \cos(2\pi n x) \, \mathrm dx \\ &= 2\int_{0}^{1}\ln\left(\frac{\pi}{\sin(\pi x)\Gamma(1-x)}\right)\cos(2\pi n x) \, \mathrm dx, \, \text{via gamma reflection} \\ &= 2\bigg(\underbrace{\int_{0}^{1} \ln \pi \cos(2\pi n x) \, \mathrm dx}_{=0, \text{ orthogonality}} - \int_{0}^{1}\ln \sin (\pi x) \cos(2\pi nx) \, \mathrm dx - \underbrace{\int_{0}^{1}\ln\Gamma(1-x) \cos(2\pi n x) \, \mathrm dx}_{=a_{n}, \text{ via } t=1-x}\bigg) \\ &= - \int_{0}^{1}\ln \sin (\pi x) \cos(2\pi nx) \, \mathrm dx \\ &= -\frac{1}{\pi}\int_{0}^{\pi}\ln \sin t \cos(2nt) \, \mathrm dt, \, \text{via }t=\pi x \\ &= \frac{1}{2\pi n}\int_{0}^{\pi} \cot t \sin(2nt) \, \mathrm dt,\, \text{via integration by parts.} \end{aligned} $$ Noting the algebraic identity $$ \cot t = i - \frac{2i}{1-e^{2it}}, $$ we thus obtain $$\begin{aligned} a_{n} &= \frac{1}{2\pi n}\int_{0}^{\pi} \left(i - \frac{2i}{1-e^{2it}}\right) \sin(2nt) \, \mathrm dt \\ &= \frac{1}{2\pi n}\left[i\underbrace{\int_{0}^{\pi}\sin(2nt) \, \mathrm dt}_{=0, \text{ orthogonality}} - 2i\int_{0}^{\pi}\frac{\sin(2nt)}{1-e^{2it}} \, \mathrm dt \right].\end{aligned} $$ We carefully apply the geometric series to the remaining integral: $$\begin{aligned} \int_{0}^{\pi}\frac{\sin(2nt)}{1-e^{2it}} \, \mathrm dt &= \int_{0}^{\pi}\sin(2nt)\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}e^{2ikt}\, \mathrm dt \\ &= \underbrace{\sum_{\substack{k=0 \\ k \neq n}}^{\infty}\int_{0}^{\pi}\sin(2nt)e^{2ikt} \, \mathrm dt}_{=0, \text{ orthogonality}} + \underbrace{\int_{0}^{\pi}\sin(2nt)e^{2int} \, \mathrm dt}_{=\frac{i\pi}{2}, \text{ orthogonality}} \\ &= \frac{i\pi}{2}. \end{aligned} $$ Assembling our results, $$ a_{n} = \frac{1}{2n}. $$ $b_{n}$ has been evaluated here on StackExchange. Its result is $$ b_{n} = 2\int_{0}^{1}\ln \Gamma(x) \sin(2\pi n x) \, \mathrm dx = \frac{\gamma + \ln(2\pi n)}{\pi n}, $$ where $\gamma$ denotes the Euler-Mascheroni constant. Therefore, we have for $0 < x < 1$: $$ \ln \Gamma(x) = \frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\bigg(\frac{1}{2n}\cos(2\pi n x) + \frac{\gamma + \ln(2\pi n)}{\pi n}\sin(2\pi nx)\bigg). $$ Now, we choose to evaluate the above at $x=\frac{3}{4}$. That is $$\begin{aligned} \ln \Gamma\left(\frac{3}{4}\right) &= \frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\bigg(\frac{1}{2n}\cos\left(\frac{3\pi n}{2}\right) + \frac{\gamma + \ln(2\pi n)}{\pi n}\sin\left(\frac{3\pi n}{2}\right)\bigg) \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi) + \sum_{m=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^m}{2(2m)} + \sum_{m=0}^{\infty}(-1)^{m+1}\frac{\gamma + \ln(2\pi(2m+1))}{\pi(2m+1)} \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi) + \frac{1}{4}\underbrace{\sum_{m=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^m}{m}}_{=-\ln 2} + \frac{\gamma + \ln(2\pi)}{\pi}\underbrace{\sum_{m=0}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{m+1}}{2m+1}}_{=-\frac{\pi}{4}} + \frac{1}{\pi}\underbrace{\sum_{m=0}^{\infty}(-1)^{m+1}\frac{\ln(2m+1)}{2m+1}}_{=\beta'(1)} \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\ln(2\pi) - \frac{1}{4}\ln 2 - \frac{\gamma + \ln(2\pi)}{4} + \frac{1}{\pi}\beta'(1), \end{aligned} $$ and we can solve for $\beta'(1)$ and simplify to conclude that $$ \beta'(1) = \pi \ln\Gamma\left(\frac{3}{4}\right) + \frac{\pi\gamma}{4} - \frac{\pi}{4}\ln \pi. $$
