What is the value of $$ \int_{0}^{1}\frac{\arctan\left(\sqrt{x^2+2}\right)}{\sqrt{x^2+2}\left(x^2+1\right)} \, \mathrm dx? $$

We want to evaluate Ahmedâ€™s integral: $$ I = \int_{0}^{1}\frac{\arctan\left(\sqrt{x^2+2}\right)}{\sqrt{x^2+2}\left(x^2+1\right)} \, \mathrm dx. $$ We first introduce a parameter $a \geq 0$ as $$ J(a) = \int_{0}^{1}\frac{\arctan\left(a\sqrt{x^2+2}\right)}{\sqrt{x^2+2}\left(x^2+1\right)} \, \mathrm dx, $$ where we are looking for $I=J(1)$. We differentiate both sides with respect to $a$, decompose the result into partial fractions and evaluate the integrals: $$\begin{aligned} J'(a) &= \int_{0}^{1}\frac{1}{\left(x^2+1\right)\left(a^2x^2+2a^2+1\right)} \, \mathrm dx \\ &= \frac{1}{a^2+1}\left(\int_{0}^{1}\frac{1}{x^2+1} \, \mathrm dx - \int_{0}^{1}\frac{a^2}{a^2x^2+2a^2+1} \, \mathrm dx \right) \\ &= \frac{1}{a^2+1}\left(\frac{\pi}{4} - \int_{0}^{1}\frac{1}{x^2+\sqrt{\frac{2a^2+1}{a^2}}^2} \, \mathrm dx \right) \\ &= \frac{1}{a^2+1}\left[\frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{a}{\sqrt{2a^2+1}}\arctan\left(\frac{a}{\sqrt{2a^2+1}}\right) \right]. \end{aligned} $$ Now, we integrate both sides from $1$ to $\infty$: $$ J(\infty) - J(1) = \frac{\pi}{4}\underbrace{\int_{1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{a^2+1} \, \mathrm da}_{A_{1}} - \underbrace{\int_{1}^{\infty}\frac{a\arctan\left(\frac{a}{\sqrt{2a^2+1}}\right)}{\sqrt{2a^2+1}\left(a^2+1\right)} \, \mathrm da}_{A_{2}}. $$ We clearly have $$ A_{1} = \int_{1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{a^2+1} \, \mathrm da = \frac{\pi}{4}, $$ and then $$\begin{aligned} A_{2} &= \int_{1}^{\infty}\frac{a\arctan\left(\frac{a}{\sqrt{2a^2+1}}\right)}{\sqrt{2a^2+1}\left(a^2+1\right)} \, \mathrm da \\ &= \int_{0}^{1}\frac{\arctan\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{t^2+2}}\right)}{\sqrt{t^2+2}\left(t^2+1\right)} \, \mathrm dt, \, \text{via } a=1/t \\ &= \frac{\pi}{2}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{1}{\sqrt{t^2+2}\left(t^2+1\right)} \, \mathrm dt - \int_{0}^{1}\frac{\arctan\left(\sqrt{t^2+2}\right)}{\sqrt{t^2+2}\left(t^2+1\right)} \, \mathrm dt, \, \text{via } \arctan(1/z) = \pi/2 - \arctan z \\ &= J(\infty) - J(1). \end{aligned} $$ Therefore, we may solve for $J(1)$: $$\displaystyle J(1) = J(\infty) - \frac{\pi^2}{32}. $$ It remains to evaluate $J(\infty)$: $$\begin{aligned} J(\infty) &= \frac{\pi}{2}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{1}{\sqrt{x^2+2}\left(x^2+1\right)} \, \mathrm dx \\ &= \frac{\pi}{2}\int_{1}^{\infty}\frac{y}{\sqrt{2y^2+1}\left(y^2+1\right)} \, \mathrm dy, \, \text{via } x=1/y \\ &= \frac{\pi}{4}\int_{1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{\sqrt{2w+1}(w+1)} \, \mathrm dw, \, \text{via } w=y^2 \\ &= \frac{\pi}{2}\int_{\sqrt{3}}^{\infty}\frac{1}{z^2+1} \, \mathrm dz, \, \text{via } z=\sqrt{2w+1} \\ &= \frac{\pi^2}{12}. \end{aligned} $$ We conclude that $$ J(1) = I = \frac{5\pi^2}{96}. $$
