What is the value of $$ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{n^2}\left(1+\frac{1}{2}+\cdots +\frac{1}{n}\right)^2? $$

We want to evaluate the infinite series $$ S = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\frac{H_{n}}{n}\right)^2 $$ where $$ H_{n} = \sum_{k=1}^{n}\frac{1}{k} $$ denotes the $n^{th}$ harmonic number.

Before we begin, we need to note some tricky double sum laws. Given a summand that is symmetric in the variables $k$ and $j$: $$ \sum_{k,j=1}^{\infty}a_{k,j} = \sum_{k,j=1}^{\infty}a_{j,k}, $$ we may rewrite the sum as $$ \sum_{k,j=1}^{\infty}a_{k,j} = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty}a_{k,k} + 2\sum_{1 \leq k < j}^{}a_{k,j}. $$ However, we also have that $$ \sum_{1 \leq k < j}^{}a_{k,j} = \sum_{k,m=1}^{\infty}a_{k,k+m}. $$ Putting together these $2$ equalities, we thus obtain $$ \sum_{k,j=1}^{\infty}a_{k,j} = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty}a_{k,k} + 2\sum_{k,m=1}^{\infty}a_{k,k+m} \tag{1.}$$ and $$ \sum_{k,m=1}^{\infty}a_{k,k+m} = \frac{1}{2}\left(\sum_{k,j=1}^{\infty}a_{k,j}-\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}a_{k,k}\right) \tag{2.}$$ where these results will be used later.

For the sum, we start by noting that via telescoping series, $$ \frac{H_{n}}{n} = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{k(k+n)}. $$ Applying this in $S$, we have $$ S = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{k(k+n)}\right)^2 = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k,j=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{kj(k+n)(j+n)}. $$ Using (1.), the above becomes $$\begin{aligned} S &= \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left(\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{k^2(k+n)^2} + 2\sum_{k,m=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{k(k+m)(k+n)(k+m+n)} \right) \\ &= \underbrace{\sum_{k,n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{k^2(k+n)^2}}_{S_{1}} + 2\underbrace{\sum_{k,m,n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{k(k+m)(k+n)(k+m+n)}}_{S_{2}}. \end{aligned} $$ For $S_{1}$, using (2.) we have $$\begin{aligned} S_{1} &= \sum_{k,n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{k^2(k+n)^2} \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\left(\sum_{k,j=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{k^2j^2} - \sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{k^4}\right) \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\left(\zeta^2(2) - \zeta(4)\right). \end{aligned} $$ For $S_{2}$, using the following identity: $$ \frac{(k+m)(k+n)-k(k+m+n)}{mn} = 1, $$ we have $$\begin{aligned} S_{2} &= \sum_{k,m,n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{k(k+m)(k+n)(k+m+n)} \\ &= \sum_{k,m,n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(k+m)(k+n)-k(k+m+n)}{kmn(k+m)(k+n)(k+m+n)} \\ &= \sum_{k,m,n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{kmn(k+m+n)} - \underbrace{\sum_{k,m,n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{mn(k+m)(k+n)}}_{=S}. \end{aligned} $$ Therefore, noting that $S = S_{1} + 2S_{2}$, we solve for $S$ as $$ S = \frac{1}{6}\left(\zeta^2(2) - \zeta(4)\right) + \frac{2}{3}\sum_{k,m,n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{kmn(k+m+n)}. $$ And finally, we evaluate the remaining sum: $$\begin{aligned} \sum_{k,m,n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{kmn(k+m+n)} &= \int_{0}^{1}\sum_{k,m,n=1}^{\infty}\frac{x^{k+m+n-1}}{kmn} \, \mathrm dx \\ &= -\int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln^3(1-x)}{x} \, \mathrm dx, \, \text{via Maclaurin series} \\ &= -\int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln^3t}{1-t} \, \mathrm dt, \, \text{via }t=1-x \\ &= -\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\int_{0}^{1}t^k\ln^3t \, \mathrm dt , \, \text{via geo. series} \\ &= 6\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{1}{(k+1)^4}, \, \text{via IBP} \\ &= 6\zeta(4). \end{aligned} $$ Putting together our results, we conclude that $$ S = \frac{17}{4}\zeta(4) = \frac{17\pi^4}{360} $$ as requested.
