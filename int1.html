What is the value of $$ \int_{0}^{1}\frac{x\ln\left(\frac{1+x}{1-x}\right)}{x^2+3} \, \mathrm dx? $$

We want to evaluate $$ I = \int_{0}^{1}\frac{x\ln\left(\frac{1+x}{1-x}\right)}{x^2+3} \, \mathrm dx. $$ To make this as simple as possible, we first quickly evaluate (for $a>-1$): $$\begin{aligned} J(a) &= \int_{0}^{1}t^a\ln t \, \mathrm dt \\ &= -\int_{0}^{\infty}we^{-(a+1)w} \, \mathrm dw, \, \text{via } w=-\ln t \\ &= -\frac{1}{(a+1)^2}\int_{0}^{\infty}se^{-s} \, \mathrm ds, \, \text{via } s = (a+1)w \\ &= -\frac{1}{(a+1)^2}, \, \text{via gamma function.} \end{aligned} $$ Now to the integral in question. First we apply the interval inverting substitution $x = \frac{1-t}{1+t}$ or equivalently $t = \frac{1-x}{1+x}$: $$\begin{aligned} I &= \int_{1}^{0}\frac{\left(\frac{1-t}{1+t}\right)\ln(1/t)}{\left(\frac{1-t}{1+t}\right)^2+3} \cdot \frac{-2}{(1+t)^2}\, \mathrm dt \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{(t-1)\ln t}{(t+1)(t^2+t+1)}\, \mathrm dt, \, \text{simplifying} \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{(2t+1)\ln t}{t^2+t+1} \, \mathrm dt - \int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln t}{t+1} \, \mathrm dt, \, \text{partial fractions.} \end{aligned} $$ Let $$\begin{aligned} I_{1} &= \int_{0}^{1}\frac{(2t+1)\ln t}{t^2+t+1} \, \mathrm dt \\ I_{2} &= \int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln t}{t+1} \, \mathrm dt. \end{aligned} $$ We work $I_{1}$. By multiplying the numerator and denominator by $1-t$ then applying geometric series, we yield $$\begin{aligned} I_{1} &= \int_{0}^{1}\frac{\left(1+t-2t^2\right)\ln t}{1-t^3} \, \mathrm dt \\ &= \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\int_{0}^{1}\left(t^{3k}+t^{3k+1}-2t^{3k+2}\right) \ln t \, \mathrm dt \\ &= \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\left[J(3k)+J(3k+1)-2J(3k+2)\right] \\ &= - \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\left[\frac{1}{(3k+1)^2} + \frac{1}{(3k+2)^2}\right] + 2\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{1}{(3k+3)^2}, \end{aligned} $$ where clearly $$ \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{1}{(3k+3)^2} = \frac{1}{9}\zeta(2). $$ To evaluate the remaining series, consider that $$\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\left[\frac{1}{(3k+1)^2} + \frac{1}{(3k+2)^2}+\frac{1}{(3k+3)^2}\right] = \zeta(2), $$ or isolating the sum in question, $$\begin{aligned} \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\left[\frac{1}{(3k+1)^2} + \frac{1}{(3k+2)^2}\right] &= \zeta(2)-\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{1}{(3k+3)^2} \\ &= \zeta(2) - \frac{1}{9}\zeta(2) \\ &= \frac{8}{9}\zeta(2). \end{aligned} $$ Therefore, assembling our results, we have $$ I_{1} = -\frac{2}{3}\zeta(2). $$ We work $I_{2}$. We have by geometric series $$\begin{aligned} I_{2} &= \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}(-1)^k\int_{0}^{1}t^k\ln t \, \mathrm dt \\ &= \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}(-1)^kJ(k) \\ &= -\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^k}{(k+1)^2} \\ &= -\frac{1}{1^2}+\frac{1}{2^2}-\frac{1}{3^2}+\frac{1}{4^2} \cdots \\ &= -\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{k^2} + 2\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{(2k)^2} \\ &= -\frac{1}{2}\zeta(2). \end{aligned} $$ Since $I = \frac{1}{2}I_{1} - I_{2}$, we conclude that $$ I = \int_{0}^{1}\frac{x\ln\left(\frac{1+x}{1-x}\right)}{x^2+3} \, \mathrm dx = \frac{1}{6}\zeta(2) = \frac{\pi^2}{36}. $$
