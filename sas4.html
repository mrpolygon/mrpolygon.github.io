What is the value of $$ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=1}^{2n}(-1)^{n+1}\frac{(2\pi)^{2n}}{(2n-k+1)!}\left(\frac{1}{2\pi i}\right)^k? $$

We want to evaluate the double sum $$ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=1}^{2n}(-1)^{n+1}\frac{(2\pi)^{2n}}{(2n-k+1)!}\left(\frac{1}{2\pi i}\right)^k. $$ We begin our journey with the definition of a monomial over $0< x < 1$ for $p=0,1,2\ldots$ such that $m_{p}(x+1)=m_{p}(x)$: $$ m_{p}(x) = x^p, $$ where we are attempting to evaluate the complex Fourier coefficients: $$ m_{p}(x) = \sum_{q=-\infty}^{+\infty}M_{q,p}e^{2\pi iqx}. $$ The Fourier coefficients are thus given by orthogonality as $$ M_{q,p} = \begin{cases} \int_{0}^{1}x^pe^{-2\pi iqx} \, \mathrm dx, & q \neq 0 \\ \int_{0}^{1}x^p \, \mathrm dx, & q = 0. \end{cases} $$ For the case where $q \neq 0$, using integration by parts we obtain the recurrence relation $$ M_{q \neq 0,p} = \begin{cases} \frac{p}{2\pi iq}M_{q \neq 0,p-1} - \frac{1}{2\pi iq}, & p \geq 1 \\ 0, & p = 0, \end{cases} $$ then we solve the above recurrence relation to obtain $$ M_{q \neq 0,p} = -\sum_{k=1}^{p}\frac{p!}{(p-k+1)!}\left(\frac{1}{2\pi iq}\right)^{k}. $$ We directly integrate for the case where $q=0$: $$ M_{q=0,p} = \frac{1}{p+1}. $$ Then altogether, $$ M_{q,p} = \begin{cases} \displaystyle -\sum_{k=1}^{p}\frac{p!}{(p-k+1)!}\left(\frac{1}{2\pi iq}\right)^{k}, & q \neq 0 \\ \displaystyle \frac{1}{p+1}, & q=0. \end{cases} \tag{*}$$ Next, we consider the Fourier series of $\cos(2\pi x)$ over $0 < x < 1$: $$ \cos(2\pi x) = \sum_{q=-\infty}^{+\infty}S_{q}e^{2\pi iqx}. $$ It is rather trivial that the Fourier coefficients are given by $$ S_{q} = \frac{1}{2}\delta[q-1] + \frac{1}{2}\delta[q+1]. $$ However, what if we instead consider Fourier expanding the Maclaurin series of $\cos(2\pi x)$: $$ \cos(2\pi x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}(-1)^n\frac{(2\pi)^{2n}}{(2n)!}x^{2n}? $$ Hence, by superposition of (*), we obtain $$\begin{aligned} S_{q \neq 0} &= \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=1}^{2n}(-1)^{n+1}\frac{(2\pi)^{2n}}{(2n-k+1)!}\left(\frac{1}{2\pi iq}\right)^{k} \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\delta[q-1] + \frac{1}{2}\delta[q+1]. \end{aligned} $$ Finally, we let $q=1$ to conclude that $$ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\sum_{k=1}^{2n}(-1)^{n+1}\frac{(2\pi)^{2n}}{(2n-k+1)!}\left(\frac{1}{2\pi i}\right)^{k} = \frac{1}{2}. $$
