What is the value of $$ \sum_{k=1}^{n}\cos\left(\frac{k\pi}{n+1}\right)?$$

Begin by using the finite geometric series (valid when $x \neq 1$) $$ \sum_{k=1}^{n}x^k = \frac{x^{n+1}-x}{x-1}, $$ then replace $x$ with $e^{i\theta}$ $$ \sum_{k=1}^{n}e^{ki\theta} = \frac{e^{(n+1)i\theta}-e^{i\theta}}{e^{i\theta}-1}. $$ Conjugate the denominator to equate real components then simplify via sum to product trigonometric identity $$\begin{aligned} \sum_{k=1}^{n}\cos(k\theta) &= \mathcal{Re}\left(\frac{e^{(n+1)i\theta}-e^{i\theta}}{e^{i\theta}-1} \cdot \frac{e^{-i\theta}-1}{e^{-i\theta}-1}\right) \\ &= \frac{\cos(n\theta)+\cos(\theta)-\cos((n+1)\theta)-1}{2-2\cos(\theta)} \\ &= \frac{2\cos\left(\frac{(n+1)\theta}{2}\right)\cos\left(\frac{(n-1)\theta}{2}\right)-\cos((n+1)\theta)-1}{2-2\cos(\theta)}. \end{aligned} $$ Finally, let $\theta=\frac{\pi}{n+1}$ to conclude that $$\begin{aligned} \sum_{k=1}^{n}\cos\left(\frac{k\pi}{n+1}\right) &= \frac{2\cos\left(\frac{(n+1)\pi}{(n+1)2}\right)\cos\left(\frac{(n-1)\pi}{(n+1)2}\right)-\cos\left(\frac{(n+1)\pi}{n+1}\right)-1}{2-2\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{n+1}\right)} \\ &= \frac{0+1-1}{2-2\cos\left(\frac{\pi}{n+1}\right)} \\ &= 0. \end{aligned} $$
