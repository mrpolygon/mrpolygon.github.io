What is the value of $$ \int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln(1-x)\ln(1+x)}{x} \, \mathrm dx? $$

We want to evaluate the integral $$ I = \int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln(1-x)\ln(1+x)}{x} \, \mathrm dx. $$ We begin by noting the algebraic identity $ab = \frac{1}{4}\left((a+b)^2-(a-b)^2\right)$. Applying this with $a=\ln(1-x)$ and $b=\ln(1+x)$ yields $$ I = \frac{1}{4}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln^2\left(1-x^2\right)}{x} \, \mathrm dx - \frac{1}{4}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln^2\left(\frac{1-x}{1+x}\right)}{x} \, \mathrm dx. $$ Now, for the first integral we let $t=x^2$ and for the latter integral we let $t=\frac{1-x}{1+x}$: $$ I = \frac{1}{8}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln^2(1-t)}{t} \, \mathrm d t - \frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln^2(t)}{1-t^2} \, \mathrm d t. $$ Additionally, for the first integral we replace $1-t$ with $t$ then to both integrals we apply the geometric series and interchange the order of integration and summation: $$\begin{aligned} I &= \frac{1}{8}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln^2(t)}{1-t} \, \mathrm d t - \frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln^2(t)}{1-t^2} \, \mathrm d t \\ &= \frac{1}{8}\int_{0}^{1}\ln^2(t)\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}t^k \, \mathrm d t - \frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{1}\ln^2(t)\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}t^{2k} \, \mathrm d t \\ &= \frac{1}{8}\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\int_{0}^{1}\ln^2(t)t^k \, \mathrm d t - \frac{1}{2}\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\int_{0}^{1}\ln^2(t)t^{2k} \, \mathrm d t . \end{aligned} $$ Via integration by parts twice, the following result is standard: $$ \int_{0}^{1}\ln^2(t)t^a \mathrm d t = \frac{2}{(a+1)^3}, \, \, \, \mathcal{Re}(a) > -1 . $$ Thus, we substitute to yield the form of the Riemann zeta function: $$\begin{aligned} I &= \frac{1}{4}\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{1}{(k+1)^3} - \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\frac{1}{(2k+1)^3} \\ &= \frac{1}{4}\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{n^3} - \left(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{n^3} - \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{(2n)^3}\right) \\ &= \frac{1}{4}\zeta(3) - \left(\zeta(3) - \frac{1}{8}\zeta(3)\right) \\ &= -\frac{5}{8}\zeta(3). \end{aligned} $$ Recognizing that $\zeta(3)$ is also known as Aperyâ€™s constant, we conclude that $$ I = \int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln(1-x)\ln(1+x)}{x} \, \mathrm dx = -\frac{5}{8}\zeta(3). $$
