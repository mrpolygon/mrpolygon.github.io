What is the value of $$ \sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{k+n}}{k^m(k+2n-1)^m} $$ for some fixed $m > 0$?

We start by mentioning $4$ important results that will be used throughout this answer. For integers $a>-1$ and $b>0$, $$ \int_{0}^{1}x^a\ln^{b-1}x \, \mathrm dx = \frac{(-1)^{b-1}}{(a+1)^b}\Gamma(b), \tag{A.}$$ where $\Gamma(b)$ references the gamma function. Also, $$\displaystyle \int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln^{b-1}x}{x^2+1} \, \mathrm dx = (-1)^{b-1}\Gamma(b)\beta(b), \tag{B.}$$ where $\beta(b)$ references the Dirichlet beta function. In a similar fashion, $$ \int_{0}^{1}\frac{x\ln^{b-1}x}{x^2+1} \, \mathrm dx = \frac{(-1)^{b-1}}{2^b}\Gamma(b)\eta(b), \tag{C.}$$ where $\eta(b)$ references the Dirichlet eta function. Finally, there is an integral identity for the polylogarithm: $$ \int_{0}^{1}\frac{x\ln^{b-1}t}{xt+1} \, \mathrm dt = (-1)^b\Gamma(b)\mathrm{Li}_{b}(-x). \tag{D.}$$ These results are rather straightforward to derive via geometric series and gamma function.

Now, we want to evaluate the double sum for some fixed integer $m > 0$: $$ S = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{k+n}}{k^m(k+2n-1)^m}. $$ Using (A.), interchanging the order of summation and integration, and applying geometric series and definition of polylogarithm, we obtain $$\begin{aligned} S &= \sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{k+n}}{k^m}\left(\frac{(-1)^{m-1}}{\Gamma(m)}\int_{0}^{1}x^{k+2n-2}\ln^{m-1}x \, \mathrm dx\right) \\ &= \frac{(-1)^{m-1}}{\Gamma(m)}\int_{0}^{1}\ln^{m-1}x\left(\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-x)^k}{k^m}\right)\left(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(-1)^nx^{2n-2}\right) \, \mathrm dx \\ &= \frac{(-1)^{m-1}}{\Gamma(m)}\int_{0}^{1}\ln^{m-1}x\bigg(\mathrm{Li}_{m}(-x)\bigg)\left(-\frac{1}{x^2+1}\right) \, \mathrm dx \\ &= \frac{(-1)^m}{\Gamma(m)}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln^{m-1}x\mathrm{Li}_{m}(-x)}{x^2+1} \, \mathrm dx. \end{aligned}$$ From here, we use (D.) and interchange the order of integration: $$\begin{aligned} S &= \frac{(-1)^m}{\Gamma(m)}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln^{m-1}x}{x^2+1} \left(\frac{(-1)^m}{\Gamma(m)}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{x\ln^{m-1}t}{xt+1} \, \mathrm dt\right) \, \mathrm dx \\ &= \frac{1}{\Gamma^2(m)}\int_{0}^{1}\ln^{m-1}t\int_{0}^{1}\frac{x\ln^{m-1}x}{\left(x^2+1\right)(xt+1)} \, \mathrm dx \, \mathrm dt. \end{aligned} $$ We decompose the inner integral into partial fractions and apply (B.), (C.), and (D.) to evaluate the integral: $$\begin{aligned} &\int_{0}^{1}\frac{x\ln^{m-1}x}{\left(x^2+1\right)(xt+1)} \, \mathrm dx \\ &= \frac{1}{t^2+1}\left[\int_{0}^{1}\frac{(t+x)\ln^{m-1}x}{x^2+1} \, \mathrm dx - \int_{0}^{1}\frac{t\ln^{m-1}x}{xt+1} \, \mathrm dx\right] \\ &= \frac{1}{t^2+1}\left[(-1)^{m-1}\Gamma(m)\beta(m)t + \frac{(-1)^{m-1}}{2^m}\Gamma(m)\eta(m) - (-1)^m\Gamma(m)\mathrm{Li}_{m}(-t)\right] \\ &= \frac{(-1)^{m-1}\Gamma(m)}{t^2+1}\left[\beta(m)t + \frac{1}{2^m}\eta(m) + \mathrm{Li}_{m}(-t)\right]. \end{aligned} $$ Therefore, using (B.) and (C.) again, $$\begin{aligned} S &= \frac{(-1)^{m-1}}{\Gamma(m)}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln^{m-1}t}{t^2+1}\left(\beta(m)t + \frac{1}{2^m}\eta(m) + \mathrm{Li}_{m}(-t)\right) \, \mathrm dt \\ &= (-1)^{m-1}\frac{\beta(m)}{\Gamma(m)}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{t\ln^{m-1}t}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt + \frac{(-1)^{m-1}}{2^m}\frac{\eta(m)}{\Gamma(m)}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{\ln^{m-1}t}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt - S \\ 2S &= (-1)^{m-1}\frac{\beta(m)}{\Gamma(m)}\cdot \frac{(-1)^{m-1}}{2^m}\Gamma(m)\eta(m) + \frac{(-1)^{m-1}}{2^m}\frac{\eta(m)}{\Gamma(m)}\cdot (-1)^{m-1}\Gamma(m)\beta(m) \\ S &= \frac{1}{2^m}\beta(m)\eta(m) \end{aligned} $$ We beautifully conclude that $$\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^{k+n}}{k^m(k+2n-1)^m} = \frac{1}{2^m}\beta(m)\eta(m). $$ It turns out this solution can be analytically continued to all real $m>0$. A proof of this is left to the interested reader.
