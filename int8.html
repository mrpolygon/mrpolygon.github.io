What is the value of $$ \int_{-\pi /6}^{\pi /6} \ln\left(\tan x + \tan\frac{\pi}{6}\right)\tan x \, \mathrm dx? $$

We want to evaluate $$ I = \int_{-\pi /6}^{\pi /6} \ln\left(\tan x + \tan\frac{\pi}{6}\right)\tan x \, \mathrm dx. $$ This one sat in my brain for a few weeks until I figured it out. That being said, it is quite involved!

First, we note that $\tan\frac{\pi}{6} = 1/\sqrt{3}$ and so applying this gives $$\begin{aligned} I &= \int_{-\pi /6}^{\pi /6} \ln\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}\tan x +1}{\sqrt{3}}\right)\tan x \, \mathrm dx \\ &= \int_{-\pi /6}^{\pi /6} \ln\left(\sqrt{3}\tan x +1\right)\tan x \, \mathrm dx - \ln \sqrt{3}\underbrace{\int_{-\pi /6}^{\pi /6} \tan x \, \mathrm dx}_{=0, \text{ odd integrand}}. \end{aligned} $$ Next we apply the substitution $t=\tan x$. This gives $$ I = \int_{-1/\sqrt{3}}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{t\ln\left(\sqrt{3}t+1\right)}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt. $$ We now introduce a paratemeter $a$ such that $$ F(a) = \int_{-1/\sqrt{3}}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{t\ln\left(\sqrt{3}t+a\right)}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt, $$ where we are looking for $I = F(1)$. Differentiating both sides with respect to $a$ then decomposing the result into partial fractions yields $$\begin{aligned} F'(a) &= \int_{-1/\sqrt{3}}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{t}{\left(t^2+1\right)\left(\sqrt{3}t+a\right)} \, \mathrm dt \\ &= \frac{1}{a^2+3}\left[\int_{-1/\sqrt{3}}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{at+\sqrt{3}}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt - \int_{-1/\sqrt{3}}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{\sqrt{3}a}{\sqrt{3}t+a} \, \mathrm dt \right]. \end{aligned} $$ We have $$\begin{aligned} \int_{-1/\sqrt{3}}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{at+\sqrt{3}}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt &= \underbrace{\int_{-1/\sqrt{3}}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{at}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt}_{=0, \text{ odd integrand}} + \int_{-1/\sqrt{3}}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{\sqrt{3}}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt \\ &= \sqrt{3}\arctan t \Bigg|_{-1/\sqrt{3}}^{1/\sqrt{3}} \\ &= \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{3}}. \end{aligned} $$ And, $$\begin{aligned} \int_{-1/\sqrt{3}}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{\sqrt{3}a}{\sqrt{3}t+a} \, \mathrm dt &= \int_{-1}^{1}\frac{a}{w+a} \, \mathrm dw , \, \text{via } w = \sqrt{3}t \\ &= a\ln(w+a)\Bigg|_{w=-1}^{w=1} \\ &= a\ln\left(\frac{a+1}{a-1}\right). \end{aligned} $$ Therefore, assembling our intermediate results: $$ F'(a) = \frac{1}{a^2+3}\left[\frac{\pi}{\sqrt{3}} - a\ln\left(\frac{a+1}{a-1}\right)\right]. $$ We now integrate both sides of the above from $0$ to $1$: $$ F(1)-F(0) = \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{3}}\int_{0}^{1}\frac{1}{a^2+3} \, \mathrm da -\int_{0}^{1}\frac{a\ln\left(\frac{a+1}{a-1}\right)}{a^2+3} \, \mathrm da. $$ We have $$ \int_{0}^{1}\frac{1}{a^2+3} \, \mathrm da = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\arctan(a/\sqrt{3}) = \frac{\pi}{6\sqrt{3}}. $$ And, $$ \int_{0}^{1}\frac{a\ln\left(\frac{a+1}{a-1}\right)}{a^2+3} \, \mathrm da = \ln(-1)\int_{0}^{1}\frac{a}{a^2+3} \, \mathrm da + \int_{0}^{1}\frac{a\ln\left(\frac{1+a}{1-a}\right)}{a^2+3} \, \mathrm da. $$ Isolating these integrals separately, we have $$ \int_{0}^{1}\frac{a}{a^2+3} \, \mathrm da = \frac{1}{2}\ln\left(a^2+3\right)\Bigg|_{0}^{1} = \frac{1}{2}\ln\left(\frac{4}{3}\right), $$ and from here we have $$ \int_{0}^{1}\frac{a\ln\left(\frac{1+a}{1-a}\right)}{a^2+3} \, \mathrm da = \frac{\pi^2}{36}. $$ Therefore, again assembling our intermediate results: $$ F(1)-F(0) = \frac{\pi^2}{36} - \frac{1}{2}\ln(-1)\ln\left(\frac{4}{3}\right). $$ It remains to evaluate $F(0)$. We start by breaking up the logarithm: $$ F(0) = \ln \sqrt{3}\underbrace{\int_{-1/\sqrt{3}}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{t}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt}_{=0, \text{ odd integrand}}+\int_{-1/\sqrt{3}}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{t\ln t}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt, $$ then we split the remaining integral into $2$ intervals like so: $$ F(0) = \int_{-1/\sqrt{3}}^{0}\frac{t\ln t}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt + \int_{0}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{t\ln t}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt. $$ In the first remaining integral, we replace $t$ with $-t$. This gives $$\begin{aligned} F(0) &= -\int_{0}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{t\ln(-t)}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt + \int_{0}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{t\ln t}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt \\ &= \ln(-1)\int_{0}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{t}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt - \int_{0}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{t\ln t}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt + \int_{0}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{t\ln t}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt \\ &= \ln(-1)\int_{0}^{1/\sqrt{3}}\frac{t}{t^2+1} \, \mathrm dt \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\ln(-1)\ln\left(t^2+1\right)\Bigg|_{0}^{1/\sqrt{3}} \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\ln(-1)\ln\left(\frac{4}{3}\right).\end{aligned} $$ In all we have that $$\begin{aligned} F(1) &= \frac{\pi^2}{36} - \frac{1}{2}\ln(-1)\ln\left(\frac{4}{3}\right)+\frac{1}{2}\ln(-1)\ln\left(\frac{4}{3}\right) \\ &= \frac{\pi^2}{36}, \end{aligned} $$ where we were careful to choose the values from $\ln(-1) = \pm i \pi$ such that the imaginary components canceled (because we know $F(1)$ is real-valued).

Take a deep breath. Since $F(1)=I$, we are done. We conclude that $$ \int_{-\pi /6}^{\pi /6} \ln\left(\tan x + \tan\frac{\pi}{6}\right)\tan x \, \mathrm dx = \frac{\pi^2}{36}. $$
